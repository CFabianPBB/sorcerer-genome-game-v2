Mars Surface Operations — 225 Million Kilometers from Earth
"If there is life on Mars, we don't need to bring it back on a rocket. We sequence its DNA on the surface, radio it back to Earth, and print it in a containment lab. Biological teleportation."
— Craig Venter

The Story

Craig Venter and Elon Musk discussed deploying a DBC on Mars. The plan: send a robotic DNA sequencer to the Martian surface, drill for microbial life in the soil, and radio any discovered genome back to Earth — where it would be printed and studied in a containment laboratory.

Venter sent his own researchers to the Mojave Desert to test autonomous soil-sequencing robots as a Mars analog. The Mojave's extremophile microbes are the closest thing on Earth to what might survive in Martian regolith. Every discovery in that desert was practice for the real mission.

The ultimate question: is life a universal phenomenon, or a miraculous accident? If Martian microbes use the same DNA base pairs as Earth life, we share a cosmic origin. If they use something different — we've found a second genesis. Either answer rewrites everything we know about life in the universe.

How to Play

  • Drive your rover across the Martian surface — click to move it
  • Drill soil samples at promising sites to collect alien material
  • Sequence samples — alien DNA takes longer to decode
  • Transmit genomes to Earth (8-minute signal delay — plan ahead!)
  • Decode alien base pairs — they may not use A, T, C, G
  • Deploy terraforming organisms to slowly transform the atmosphere
You are humanity's first exobiologist. The rover is your hands. The signal delay is your constraint. The universe's greatest question is your mission.
