CHAPTER THREE

Life at the Speed of Light

The Story

Craig Venter imagined a world where biology could be transmitted like email. His Digital-to-Biological Converter (DBC) — sometimes called a "biological teleporter" — can receive a digital DNA sequence over the internet and physically print the corresponding organism: a vaccine, an antibiotic, a synthetic cell. No shipping. No cold chain. Just data in, life out.

The vision: a global network of DBC printers stationed in hospitals, clinics, and field stations around the world. When a pandemic erupts in Lagos, the genome is sequenced, transmitted digitally to every node on the network, and within hours, vaccines are printing on-site — no planes, no customs, no delay.

"We can digitize biology, transmit it at the speed of light, and reconstruct it at the other end. We are entering an era where we can 'fax' life to anywhere on Earth — or beyond."
— Craig Venter

Your Role

You are the network operations commander for the global DBC deployment. The world map before you is your command center. Each glowing node is a DBC printer — or a city waiting for one. You must build and maintain a signal network spanning continents, keep transmissions clean, and respond instantly when outbreaks erupt.

How to Play

  • Install DBC Printers — Deploy biological converters in cities around the world, from San Diego to the Lunar Relay
  • Transmit Genomes — Send digital DNA sequences across the network to active nodes
  • Synthesize Vaccines — Print vaccines and therapeutics on-site at remote locations
  • Repair Signals — When transmissions get corrupted, click the corrupted bases to fix them before the data degrades
  • Fight Outbreaks — Respond to pandemic events that erupt at random cities — deploy vaccines before they spread
  • Upgrade Signal Arrays — Improve your network's transmission fidelity and range

The Stakes

The WHO, China's biotech program, and Big Pharma each have their own vision for how biological printing should work — and who should control it. Pandemic outbreaks don't wait for committee approvals. Every second of signal delay, every corrupted transmission, every city without a printer is a city where people die waiting. Your network is the difference between a contained outbreak and a global catastrophe.
