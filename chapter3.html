The Story

Venter's most audacious invention: a machine that receives DNA sequences over the internet and synthesizes living organisms from scratch. He called it the Digital-to-Biological Converter. In 2013 it was a tabletop prototype. Its implications were civilization-changing.

When H1N1 threatened Mexico City, Venter's team synthesized the vaccine digitally — without ever handling the live virus. When COVID variants emerged, DBC technology enabled vaccine development in days instead of months. The DBC doesn't ship medicine. It transmits the instructions to print it, anywhere, instantly.

Elon Musk contacted Venter about using the DBC to print terraforming bacteria on Mars. The vision: beam life across the solar system at the speed of light. Biology, finally, freed from geography.

How to Play

  • Install DBC printers in cities worldwide — each node expands your network
  • Transmit genomes between nodes to synthesize vaccines on demand
  • Respond to outbreak events before they become pandemics
  • Maintain signal integrity — transmission errors corrupt the genome
  • Upgrade your signal array to reach the Moon and beyond
You are the network architect. Every DBC printer you install is a node in humanity's immune system. Every successful transmission stops a disease in its tracks.
